# Amazon Leadership Principles -- Behavioral Questions

One question per Leadership Principle. Prepare your answer using STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result), then check `answers.md` for reference answers drawn from your experience.

---

## 1. Customer Obsession

**Definition:** Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you had to balance technical elegance with what the customer actually needed. How did you decide what to prioritize?

---

## 2. Ownership

**Definition:** Leaders act on behalf of the entire company, beyond just their own team. They never say "that's not my job."

**Question:** Describe a time when you took ownership of a problem that was outside your direct area of responsibility. What did you do and what was the outcome?

---

## 3. Invent and Simplify

**Definition:** Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams, and always find ways to simplify.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you built something new that significantly simplified an existing process or system. What was the innovation and how did you validate it?

---

## 4. Are Right, A Lot

**Definition:** Leaders have strong judgment and good instincts. They seek diverse perspectives and work to disconfirm their beliefs.

**Question:** Give me an example of a time you made a technical decision that others initially disagreed with, but you turned out to be right. How did you arrive at your conclusion?

---

## 5. Learn and Be Curious

**Definition:** Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves. They are curious about new possibilities and act to explore them.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you had to learn a completely new domain or skill set to be effective in your role. How did you approach it?

---

## 6. Hire and Develop the Best

**Definition:** Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion. They recognize exceptional talent and willingly move them throughout the organization.

**Question:** Describe how you've developed someone on your team. What was their trajectory and how did you contribute to their growth?

---

## 7. Insist on the Highest Standards

**Definition:** Leaders have relentlessly high standards that many people may think are unreasonably high. They continually raise the bar and drive their teams to deliver high-quality products.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you pushed back on shipping something because it didn't meet your quality bar, even when there was pressure to release.

---

## 8. Think Big

**Definition:** Leaders create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results. They think differently and look around corners for ways to serve customers.

**Question:** Describe a time you proposed a vision or architecture that was significantly more ambitious than what was originally scoped. How did you sell it and what happened?

---

## 9. Bias for Action

**Definition:** Leaders value calculated risk-taking. Speed matters in business, and many decisions are reversible and do not need extensive study.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you had to make a decision and move forward quickly without having all the data you wanted. What was the situation and how did it turn out?

---

## 10. Frugality

**Definition:** Leaders accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency, and invention.

**Question:** Give me an example of a time you achieved a significant result with limited resources -- budget, headcount, compute, or time. How did you maximize impact under those constraints?

---

## 11. Earn Trust

**Definition:** Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully. They are vocally self-critical, even when doing so is awkward or embarrassing.

**Question:** Describe a time you had to deliver difficult or unpopular feedback to a stakeholder or team member. How did you handle the conversation and maintain the relationship?

---

## 12. Dive Deep

**Definition:** Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, and audit frequently. No task is beneath them.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you dug deep into the data or code to find a root cause that others had missed. What did you discover?

---

## 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit

**Definition:** Leaders are obligated to respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree, even when doing so is uncomfortable or exhausting. Once a decision is determined, they commit wholly.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you disagreed with a senior leader or a team consensus on a technical direction. How did you handle it?

---

## 14. Deliver Results

**Definition:** Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion. Despite setbacks, they rise to the occasion and never settle.

**Question:** Tell me about the most impactful project you've delivered in terms of measurable business results. Walk me through the challenges and how you drove it to completion.

---

## 15. Strive to Be Earth's Best Employer

**Definition:** Leaders work every day to create a safer, more productive, higher-performing, more diverse, and more just work environment.

**Question:** Describe something you've done to make your team's work environment better -- whether that's process, culture, inclusion, or day-to-day experience.

---

## 16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

**Definition:** Leaders are determined to make better, do better, and be better for customers, employees, partners, and the world at large.

**Question:** Tell me about a time you had to consider the broader impact of your work beyond just the immediate business metric. How did you balance competing concerns?
